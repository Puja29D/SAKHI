How to Listen

Your child needs to know they are believed and not blamed.

Say this: "I believe you. I am here for you. It's not your fault."
Avoid this: "Why didn't you tell me sooner?" or "What were you wearing?"

Immediate Safety

  • Is the offender still accessible to the child? Ensure physical separation.
  • Does the child need urgent medical care? (Bleeding, pain, recent incident).
  • Preserve evidence if the incident was recent (do not wash clothes/bathe if possible).
